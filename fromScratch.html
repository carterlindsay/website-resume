My Mission:
The Dominican Republic

Santiago · Sister Training Leader · 18 Months of Service

I served as a full-time missionary in Santiago, Dominican Republic from July 2024 to December 2025 — one of the most transformative 18 months of my life. I led and trained sister missionaries, discovered a vibrant new culture, and grew in faith and character more than I ever expected.

Below is a window into the beautiful country that became my second home. ¡Que viva la República Dominicana!

✈ Best Places to Visit

  1. Beach & Coast
    • Punta Cana — world-famous white sand beaches and resort paradise on the eastern coast
    • Samaná — breathtaking cliffs, hidden beaches, and humpback whale watching every winter
  2. Cities & Culture
    • Santiago — my mission city, warm and vibrant with wonderful people
    • Santo Domingo — the capital, rich with colonial history and architecture
  3. Mountains & Nature
    • Puerto Plata — colorful Victorian architecture, a cable car, and stunning ocean views
    • Jarabacoa — the "City of Everlasting Spring," mountain rafting and waterfalls

🍳 Foods & Drinks to Try

Traditional Dishes

  • La Bandera — rice, beans & meat; the beloved national dish eaten daily
  • Sancocho — hearty stew with root vegetables and meat, served at celebrations
  • Mangú — mashed plantains with pickled onions, a breakfast staple everywhere
  • Tostones — twice-fried green plantain slices, crispy and delicious

Drinks

  • Morir Soñando — literally "die dreaming" — creamy orange juice and milk; absolutely incredible
  • Kola Real — the beloved local cola, a Dominican classic at every meal
  • Mabí — a traditional fermented bark drink, sweet and unique
